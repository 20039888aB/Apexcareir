# Society of Interventional Radiology (SIR) Overview

SIR resources describe Interventional Radiology (IR) as minimally invasive, image-guided care used for diagnosis and treatment.

## Core Principles

- Select interventions based on clinical indication and imaging findings.
- Prioritize patient safety, informed consent, and multidisciplinary decision-making.
- Use risk-stratified pathways for anticoagulation, contrast safety, and peri-procedural monitoring.
- Escalate urgent cases rapidly when red-flag symptoms are present.

## Common SIR-aligned Procedure Families

- Vascular recanalization (angioplasty, stenting, thrombectomy)
- Embolization (bleeding control, tumor therapy, symptom control)
- Image-guided biopsy and drainage
- Portal and venous interventions
- Dialysis access salvage and maintenance
